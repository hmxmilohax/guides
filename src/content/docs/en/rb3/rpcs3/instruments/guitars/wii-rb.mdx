* There is no input profile as all controls are default
* Rock Band 1 guitars **require their respective guitar dongles to connect to your computer.**  
Rock Band 1 guitars have a maple (cream color) neck with a fully white dongle.  
![White Nintendo Wii Rock Band Guitar Dongle](/assets/instruments/adapters/wiiguitarrb1.png "White Nintendo Wii Rock Band Guitar Dongle")
Model#: `WGTSELEA1B`  
* Rock Band 2 (and later) guitars **require their respective guitar dongles to connect to your computer.**  
Rock Band 2 and The Beatles: Rock Band guitar dongles are compatible with either revisions.    
![White Nintendo Wii Rock Band 2 Guitar Dongle with black sync button](/assets/instruments/adapters/wiiguitarrb2.png "White Nintendo Wii Rock Band 2 Guitar Dongle with black sync button")
Model#: `PGTSELEA2B`  
![Grey Nintendo Wii Rock Band 2 Guitar Dongle](/assets/instruments/adapters/wiiguitarrb2gry.png "Grey Nintendo Wii Rock Band 2 Guitar Dongle")
Model#: `PGTSELEA2B`  
![White Nintendo Wii The Beatles: Rock Band Guitar Dongle](/assets/instruments/adapters/wiiguitarbeatles.png "White Nintendo Wii The Beatles: Rock Band Guitar Dongle")
Model#: `PGTSELEA3B`

<details>
<summary>How do I map my guitar?</summary>
**Click on the `Pads` icon at the top of RPCS3**.  
<Image src={rpcs3pad} alt="The Pads menu in RPCS3." />

It’s suggested to create a new configuration by clicking `Add Configuration` at the top right of the `Pads` window.  
This is so you can change configuration profiles in case you want to play other games.  
<Image src={rpcs3padprofadd} alt="The top right of RPCS3's Pads window. 'Add Configuration' is being clicked on by the mouse cursor" />
Follow the instructions and/or match the assignments below.  
<Image src={padlegend} alt="The instrument page being used as a as a mapping reference for RPCS3." /></details>
